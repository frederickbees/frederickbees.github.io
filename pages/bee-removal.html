Is it actually honey bees?

A quick ID before you call — it saves everyone time.

Honey bees look like…

  • Fuzzy, amber-to-brown, about the size of a thumbnail
  • Living in a single tight cluster the size of a football or basketball
  • Hanging on a branch, fence, mailbox, or grill cover
  • Calm — not flying at you, not aggressive

Probably not honey bees if…

  • The insects are glossy / shiny with bold black-and-yellow stripes
  • They're nesting in the ground or inside a wall cavity with a small, defensible opening
  • They're dive-bombing you or your pet
  • You see a papery gray nest — that's a wasp or hornet

What happens when we arrive

  1. A volunteer with collection gear will confirm they're honey bees.
  2. They'll brush or shake the cluster into a temporary box, making sure the queen goes in.
  3. The bees get rehomed to a proper hive — often the volunteer's apiary.
  4. You get to tell this story at dinner parties for months.
