## Our mission

To provide support and information to current and future beekeepers, to
educate the public about the honey bee, and to advocate for the practice
of responsible beekeeping in Frederick County, Maryland.

## When we meet

FCBA holds general meetings on the **second Thursday of each month at
7:00 PM** at the Walkersville Library. Meetings typically include a
short business portion, a featured speaker or topic discussion, and a
raffle (usually for honey, occasionally for slightly eccentric hive
tools).

Guests are always welcome. No membership required to attend — come see
what we're like before deciding.

## How we're organized

The club is run entirely by volunteer members. An elected board of
officers handles day-to-day operations, and committees drive the
specific programs: Bee School, Swarm Response, Mentorship, Fair, and
Communications.

Our bylaws are available as a [PDF]({{ site.club.bylaws_pdf | relative_url }}).

## History

FCBA was founded in {{ site.established_year }} by a handful of
Frederick County beekeepers who wanted a place to swap stories,
equipment, and hard-won wisdom. Fifty years later, we do exactly the
same thing — just with better mite treatments and slightly faster
internet.
